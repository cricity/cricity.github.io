Presentation at the 26th APDR Congress in Aveiro,Portugal

Paulo Castro Seixas, Eunice Castro Seixas and Inês Barbosa presented papers at the 26th APDR (Portuguese Association for Regional Development / Associação Portuguesa para o Desenvolvimento Regional) Congress ( “Evidence-based territorial policymaking: formulation, implementation and evaluation of policy”, held in the University of Aveiro (Portugal), 4-5 July 2019.

Paulo Castro Seixas (Chair) and Eunice Castro Seixas organized the Special Session SS02: Children as City Experts. Contributions for Social Theory and Urban Planning whose program and call can be found below.

SS02: Children as City Experts. Contributions for Social Theory and Urban Planning
Chair: Paulo Seixas

1086 Brincadeiras, interações e apropriação do espaço público pelas crianças: estudo de caso etnográfico em dois parques urbanos do Porto. Inês Barbosa, João Teixeira Lopes, Lígia Ferro.

1130 Involving Children in Urban Planning: A Systematic Review. Eunice Castro Seixas, Maria Fernandes Jesus.

1179 Direito à Infância/Direito à Cidadania. Maria Correia.

1135 Small & Big Data: A Participação das Crianças em Políticas Públicas e Ferramentas de Apoio à Decisão. Paulo Seixas, Luís Baptista.

Special Session (SS02) on: Children as City Experts. Contributions for Social Theory and Urban Planning

Organizers:

Paulo Castro Seixas (Chair) – CAPP - Universidade de Lisboa, Portugal. E-mail: pseixas@iscsp.ulisboa.pt
Eunice Castro Seixas – Socius/ISEG – Universidade de Lisboa, Portugal. E-mail: euniceseixas@gmail.com; euniceseixas@socius.iseg.ulisboa.pt

The aim and scope:

Cities meet several challenges (development, inequities and governance) and a diversity of dilemmas: globalization and the individuals; narratives of citizens, of science, of public policies and of the global rankings…; identity and feedforwarding; big data and small data…; technological megamachines and degrowth possibilities.

From a Citizen Science paradigm, children are both city experts and symptoms of how urban planning is coping with its challenges and dilemmas. Supported by a FCT funded project (CRiCity) and in participatory/collaborative methodologies, this session places children in the core of social theory and public policies discussions. How children voices may/should be attending in urban planning? The panel is open to contributions:

- Collaborative Methodologies with Children in Cities
- Experiences and Evaluation in Child-Friendly Cities
- Right to the City, Public Spaces, Children and Urban Planning

Keywords: Children; Child-Friendly City; Citizen Science; Right to the City; Collaborative
Methodologies; Urban Planning
